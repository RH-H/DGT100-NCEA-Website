Castlepoint Scenic Reserve

Castlepoint is a small beach village on New Zealand's Wairarapa coast in the Wellington region.

It is home to a lighthouse located near the top of a reef's northern end.

The reef stretches for roughly a kilometre. Due to its enormous population of seagulls, an island on the southern end of the reef is known as "seagull island."

Christmas Bay is located on Castle Rock's southern side. Masterton is around one hour's drive from Castlepoint.



In December 2017 the lighthouse was controversially installed with multi-colour LED up-lights which would light the exterior of the lighthouse all year round at a cost of $35,000.

Many Wairarapa region residents feel the lights are 'gaudy' and a source of unnecessary light pollution.
